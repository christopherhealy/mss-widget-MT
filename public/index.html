What the widget measures

ESL Success assessments are designed for teachers who need fast, consistent signals they can use for placement, progress tracking, and lesson planning. Students record a short answer (30‚Äì60 seconds), and the widget returns a compact report across core speaking dimensions.

CEFR level estimate

A practical proficiency band to support placement and progress tracking.

Overall speaking score

A single headline score for quick comparisons across students and time.

Fluency

Flow, pausing, and speed‚Äîuseful for identifying practice priorities.

Pronunciation

Clarity, intelligibility, and pronunciation accuracy signals.

Grammar

Sentence control, error frequency, and complexity indicators.

Vocabulary

Range and appropriateness of word choice for the task.

Transcript

Auto transcript for teacher review and student feedback loops.

WPM (words per minute)

A simple fluency companion metric that helps spot pacing issues.

Task fit

Teachers can align prompts to course goals (placement, interview, TOEFL-style, etc.).

Notes: These signals are designed for teaching decisions (placement + progress). They are not a replacement for high-stakes testing, but they are reliable enough to support instruction at a fraction of the cost.
